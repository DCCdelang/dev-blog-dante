For the last three years I am working as a consultant on AI, BI and engineering topics. Coming from a background in AI and Computational Science, my job fits my background. However, I still feel like something is missing.

When looking around for other job opportunities, I find it difficult to pinpoint what it is I am looking for. As part of this journey I decided to just start applying again. For one AI engineering role I was able to go through all three interview rounds but ended up not getting an offer, which actually feels like a relief. Which reminded me of a very knowledgeable person once mentioning to me that ***"Rejection is redirection"***. 

I think you can take this in two ways. At first you could say this an easy way out when you have not succeeded and perhaps even work demotivating and give it up easily. I think however, you should read it more as a "follow your gut" instinct. I took the interviews very serious, however I noticed during the rounds that what the company is focussing on (full-throttle on AI) is not aligned with my personal vision.

This may sound strange from someone with a background in AI and that is working as a Data Scientist, but I don't think AI should be the only solution. Which is the feeling I sometimes get when looking at job descriptions or company goals. Don't get me wrong, I think AI can be a very helpful extension and speed up tedious processes.
